Domain names and ICANN

A domain name is basically just your address on the web. It's where people can find you, and it is your online identity. Businesses typically register domain names with their name and sometimes also register their product names. Individuals often register family names or names that have a private interest to them. Domain names have two parts: the label and the extension, or top-level domain, separated by a "dot." There are over 20 name extensions to settle on from. Popular name extensions such as .COM and .NET are typically employed by for-profit companies, while .ORG is usually used by a non-profit organization. Other generic extensions like .INFO and .BIZ have also more recently become available. You'll also be able to register domain names that are specific to a particular country. For example, the extensions the extensions .US, .UK, and .DE correspond to the United States, United Kingdom, and Germany, respectively.
